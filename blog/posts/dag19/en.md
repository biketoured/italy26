# The day in short
I started the day in Catania. Ironically I planned to ride up Etna, the third hardest climb in all of Italy(!). But the temperature was 23 already at 7 in the morning so I decided it would be wiser to skip the climb and head towards Calabria today.
On my way to Messina I met Umberto, 64 who works at Lavazza (very stereotypical italian job!☕🇮🇹) and he told me there is a legend that the big lava pillars on the coastline outside Acireale were thrown by a giant from the vulcano. Etna's presence in Northeastern Sicily is clear and strong. It was a great sight to see, especially as a backdrop on the narrow streets that led in its direction. <br>
I stopped by at some local cafe and I also stopped for a cooling swim (embarrassingly the first on the entire trip!). As I reached Messina I took the ferry straight away. On the other side of the sound I struggled to find a B&B but after asking a pizza baker he found a great solution. In Italy it is almost always better to ask people than to ask your phone.

## How did my body feel?
Strong in the climbs but a bit heavy legs. A little bit overheated in the afternoon but felt better after the swim.

## Best moment of the day
The Acireale - Taormina strip was beautiful and nice to bike, even though there was traffic.

[photo:1]

## Person I met
I didn't feel like talking much today, just wanted to escape the heat of Sicily and get to Calabria. But Umberto was a nice companion for 40min or so. Grazie ancora!

## What did I eat?
Some light hotel breakfast. Some local pastries, two gelato cones, cookies and candy... A pizza Messinese.

## Landscape & nature
Heavily influenced by the vulcano. Rich fertile soil with lots of lush green plants and orchards. The coast is rugged with lava rocks and dark-cokoured cliffs. The landscape looks oddly shaped. Didn't get to see the craters and real vulcano terrain though as I only kept myself between 0-700 masl. Etna is 3300m tall and I originally planned to get to 1800 masl where the landscape supposedly looks like another planet.

[photo:2]

## Smells & details
Much traffic, fumes and smoke. I also noticed that many houses have a water cannister on top of the roof to warm up water for showering and similar. I know it's common in Southern countries but hadn't seen it in this scale before.

## How do I feel overall?
Sicily has been great, especially the central and Southern parts!
The heat has taken some motivation away for now, but tomorrow I'm on a new region and I'll probably be handling the temperature better! Looking forward to what's coming!
