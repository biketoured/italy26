# The day in short
The day started at the Messina strait where I had a classic Italian breakfast: an espresso and a pastry. This energy didn't last me long so I bought some fresh fruits already after 15km and ate them on top of the first of many climbs this day would offer.<br>
When the sun was blazing hot I reached the main stop of the day, Tropea, which is perched on top of a 40m high cliff just by the ocean. This little town has it all; Beach, picturesque views, pretty buildings and good food. I ordered a pizza-like plate with N'duja and was really not disappointed. N'duja is a spicy pepperoni-salami spread that has been developed over many years by the impoverished Calabrian farmers.
It can be eaten on bread or pizza, or as a condiment. 
The rest of the day was spent on indulging the beautiful coastline views along the Calabrian peninsula. When I entered a bar in a tiny village all of the locals started talking with me; it feels like I'm on a movie sometimes. In the evening I found a nice B&B with a pizzeria attached. PERFECT! This evening also happened to be the most important football game of the year because Catanzaro was about to play off against Monza for a spot on Serie A. The pizzeria/bar/B&B was full of life until Catanzaro unfortunately lost.
Sweden is not a real football country after what I have seen.😅

## Landscape & nature
The landscape in Calabrian coast is much about forest-covered hills. This area receives much more rain than Sicily. Calabria has sand beaches all along the coastline.

[photo:2]

## Smells & details
Calabria has lots of really old olive trees, some of them several hundred years old. They grow only a couple cm in diameter each year. The air feels much fresher here than on the Sicilian coast, probably due to less traffic and industries.
