# The day in short
The day started in the tent by a beautiful lake. Immediately I was met by a 500m climb, after which I treated myself with two ice creams. The local people in this village were truly great, giving me recommendations for nice roads for the day.
I entered Abruzzo National Park, famous for bears and wolves. After another series of climbs I reached a kiosk where there was a crossbreed between wolf and dog. Apparently, the dogwolf had searched for shelter under the kiosk building because it had conceived a puppy.
When I arrived the puppy was howling (very cute) and it must have been only a week old. 
<br>
<br> Anyway, I continued climbing and eventually I reached a plateau where they have a ski park and ski lifts. The temperature was only 18 up here at 1600m.a.s.l, and I appreciated the cloudy weather after many weeks of mostky sunshine.
Thereafter I descended into Sulmone, famous for their candy and their ancient poet Ovidius. I bought a kilogramm of candy because I woukd need it if I want to bike up Blockhaus tomorrow. One of the poets most famous quotes is:
<br><br>
"Dripping water hollows out stone, not by force but by persistence."
<br><br>
This is the motto I have to big challenges. Tomorrow I'm trying the famous 1500m climb. If I'm going to make it, it has to be by persistent and intentional pedal strokes, bit by bit, compartmentalizing the goal into subgoals makes it easier. Having the next corner, curve or village as the next goal makes it easy to see the progress, while having 4000km as the end goal makes it impossible to see the progress... It is usually better to break down your goals into subparts that you can handle easier.
<br> I was also approached by a couple from Arizona who also like to travel by bike. They told me about the bike-friendly winter and spring climate in Arizona and I was very welcome if I ever go there.

## Weather
Mostly cloudy with some brief periods of sunshine. In the evening I could see some rain from the view of tge mountain.

## How did my body feel?
A little tired after a moderate sleep but I had some nutritional food for recovery so I felt relatively fresh in my legs.

## Best moment of the day
Probably no special moment but the whole experience of the largely untouched and massive foresty mountains of Abruzzo national park was nice.

The Arizonian couple. A shopkeeper and his friend who gave me useful information and road recommendations. A souvenir seller in Scanna also gave me useful info.
Carlo, who found a place to sleep in the mountain village where I couldn't find anything last minute! And Carlos brother was great to talk with while waiting for Denise to come.

## What did I eat?
Italian breakfast, grilled sandwich with Prosiutto and Scamorza, Pan d'Orso, lots of candy, three ice creams and a nice pizza with mozzarella di bufala and prosciutto. Also ate some prosciutto from Molise before sleeping.

## Landscape & nature
Very hilly and mountainous, with surprisingly much lush leafy forest even at 1600m altitude. Some deep pine forests as well. There were also big patches of just grass. In the distance I could see snow-capped mountains.

## Smells & details
Fresh mountain air, so much better than the cities!

## How do I feel overall?
Not too tired despite 2600m elevation! I was always in zone 2 and mostly light gears, so it is light work all day. Nothing too heavy.
