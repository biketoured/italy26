This morning I woke up to birdsong and a marvellous view over les Calanques de Piana. The weather forecast looked promising and I was not too tired from yesterday's 180km. <br>
I set out for breakfast at Piana and continued towards Bonfacio, the southernmost town where I will take a ferry to Sardinia. I made less progress than I wanted due to headwinds and bad road surfaces. <br>
When I reached Ajaccio I paid a visit to the one and only Napoleon Bonaparte. His statue overlooked the capital of Corsica from a hilltop. For klunch I had such a bad timing (many places close at 15 for a siesta) so I ended up eating a simple pizza...<br>

## The language 
After two days in Corsica I have gathered some information about the language. Corsica was Genoese in the middle ages, and as a result Corsican is a funny mix between Italian and French. Some areas still have a strong Italian heritage, like Calvi who's motto was *Citta Calvi, Semper Fidelis* which means City of Calvi, always faithful (towards Genoa).
Most street signs have both Corsican and French. Since I can speak Italian well and also a little French, I could understand much of the Corsican language. I think it's a cool language, they use J and U more than Italian and French.
