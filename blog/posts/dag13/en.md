.
This day started with a visit to Gairo and Gairi Vecchio. Gairo Vecchio is one of Italy's most fanous ghost towns (a town with no inhabitants).
This is because Gairo Vecchio was repeatedly affected by mud slides, floodings and similar. A flooding in 1951 was so bad that the viklage was abandoned. From the other side of the valley I could see how close the new Gairo is to the old Gairo, probably only a stone's throw away. <br>
Another fascinating fact is that this area I explored today is the core if the Sardinian "blue zone", where there is an unusually high proportion of 100-year olds. There are five blue zones in the world and uniquely for Sardinia, the unusually high living expectancy is also true for men.
The high life expectancy is accredited partly to the food
