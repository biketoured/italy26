# The day in short
After a great stay at Casa Valkegård in Arsita I headed off over the foothills of Gran Sasso towards Ascoli Piceno and the national park of Sibellini mountains. 
This area has lots of great views and I often stopped to take in the views of the "Sleeping Beauty" that takes shape in the Gran Sasso from certain angles.<br><br>
After chatting with the bakers in Ascoli Piceno, waiting for the sun to wane in power, I started making my way towards the grand 25km long hill that would take the entire afternoon. A very slight incline soon became more substantial as I approached the Sibillini national park. I could of course write a dramatic text about the climb but all I remember is that I took it in a moderate pace and stopped for a refreshing coca cola. However, the last push was actually steep with 10% for a couple kilometres!<br>
<br>
When I finally reached the mountain pass I was baffled by the beautiful valley in front of my eyes: Casteluccio on a mini hill in the distance with lots of green fields around it. No trees at all, it looked so clean and minimalistic! I ordered a lentil soup and arrosticini (roasted lamb on small sticks 🍡). It tasted amazing. Two friendly men in the staff told me two interesting things: This valley is iften the coldest place in Italy outside of the Alps, and they also explained why Italy has become worse at football, even though it hurts for them to admit it. Like many other Italians, they watch the World Championship anyway, and repeat the old devise: may the best team win.

## Weather
Sunny, 29°C in the lowlands and 19°C in the mountains. The night temperature was about 8°C.

## How did my body feel?
In the end of the day my legs felt like concrete, in a bad way. Hopefully the protein-rich food gets me recovered!

## Best moment of the day
Enjoying the views over the valley after 3200 meters of climbing throughout the day! Well deserved.

## What did I eat?
A nice breakfast at Arsita with Roger, and then I lived on candy for 90km. I  Ascoli Piceno I had some nice pastries and a sturdy vegetable-stuffed panino. Coca Cola halfway up the climb. Lentil soup and arrosticini in Casteluccio, very locally sourced since they grow lentils and herd sheep up here at 1500m elevation.

## Landscape & nature
The hills along Grand Sasso are very beautiful with a mosaic landscape of farms, forests, pastures and fields. The foresty mountains of Sibillini very impressively tall, and the valley of Castelluccio is extremely flat and picturesque. I was one week too early to see the lentils bloom in red, white and yellow, but I could see some pictures of it. Beautiful.
