I started the day slow at the hotel I found the night before. The road to Bonifacio was eastward and for the first time I could enjoy some tailwinds.
The ferry took 50 minutes — Sardinia and Corsica are rather close! After a delicious ice cream I started my Sardinia étape.
Sardinian roads are smoother and less hilly

## How did my body feel?
Recovered. I also avoided the strongest hours of sunshine by taking the ferry midday.

## Best moment of the day
Probably the evening ride on Sardinia. Good winds, nice views and just the right temperature.

[photo:1]

## Person I met
Today I mostly spoke with French tourists and similar. Many retired. They say its refreshing to see a young man going on such an adventure. I got to practice my French one last day before Italy.

## What did I eat?
A plain French hotel breakfast, a luxury fish dish at Bonifacio, and a luxury dinner at La Cerra Agricamp which was perfect stay; 40€ for a camping spot, dinner, and breakfast!

## Landscape & nature
Landscape in Corsica is rugged and never flat. Sardinia is also rugged but has much more flat ground for pastures, vineyards and fields. It is and it also feels like an older mountains, eroded bit by bit by time.

[photo:2]

## Smells & details
The mountains in Sardinia also look kind of broken. Many mountain tops are rounded or consist of boulders. The plants and trees are vastly different here and the soil is more sandy. I saw a bunch of trees with trunks that look like cork.

## How do I feel overall?
Happy to be here!
