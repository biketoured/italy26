# The day in short
I started the day in Polignano a Mare, the city who has no fear of the sea. It sits perched just on the cliffs 20 meters above the sea. Very beautiful.
I listened to a street musician sing songs I could recognise, in such a beautiful way that I cried. I wish I knew his name so I could listen to more on Spotify.
<br> I made my way towards Bari. Just 5km before Bari I bought some shrimps at a local fishery. I had never tasted fresh shrimps before and they tasted very buttery, completely different from the frozen ones I'm used to from the supermarkets in Sweden.
Bari was a beautiful town with a big historical center. I bought a focaccia, a realistic lemon pastry and some ice cream in the pretty suburb of Carbonara. All of them tasted amazing.
After riding through olive and almond fields I finally reached a high plateau where it was more grain, wheat and barley, on the fields. I had reached Altamura, the Italian capital of bread! I bought some delicious focaccia again and some cantuccini with whole milk. Very tasty!
After a stressful search for accommodation I found a great hostel in Matera, and flew through the fields the last 25km as the sun set. After a well-needed shower in the hostel I went out to experience Matera at night. It was utterly beautiful, the stone houses in the valley made for a gorgeous view!

<br><br>
Matera is in fact one of the three towns that have been inhabited for the longest time on earth. They have found traces of civilization from 9000 years ago, and it was the first city in Italy.
I recommend those who are interested to read about Matera, a very fascinating city!!

## How did my body feel?
Getting a bit stronger each day. But felt some strain in my legs after a heavy climb before Altamura.

## Best moment of the day
Polignano a mare in the morning or Matera by night, can't decide!
