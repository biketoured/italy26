Less than a month to departure, I still cannot fully comprehend it is actually happening.

[photo:1]

## What do I want to experience?

I want to see Italy's true colours, the Italian soul, not just the tourist attractions. I'm talking about the backcountry roads, depopulating villages, and local trattorias. The dialects, the architecture, the local dishes. The contrast between north and south. <br>
I also want to share stories with people along the way. I have noticed a wide range of people are much more open-minded and curious when someone comes by alone on a bicycle. A wide range of people I met while bikepacking in Sweden and Norway have expressed desire to do something similar, and despite being strangers the conversations often feel authentic and meaningful. I would love to create many moments like these, listen to locals talk about anything between their daily chores and life's challenges. Maybe I will get a taste of how Italian life is like.
I want to try something new along the way, and I also want to try some kind of competition and step outside my comfort zone. I would like to taste local delicacies, listen to street musicians, and help people whenever I can. Basically to do 'side-quests', as if this entire project wasn't one :). Most of all though, I will definitively eat lots of gelato and apply loads of sunscreen!

[photo:2]

## Special expectations
I tend to keep my expectations low to avoid feeling disappointed. However, when planning this journey I cannot help but daydream and imagine different things that I wish would happen. Perhaps I will be invited to someones home and experience somet authentic italian family dinner. 
When passing by Monaco I want to take a couple laps around the streets, and possibly capture a photo of luxury cars stalled on the roadsides and boats docked at the harbour. Hopefully I will experience the contrast between rural countryside and bustling cities, between beaches and mountains, between the old and the new whatever that means... To get new perspectives.
If possible, I would ride up part of the Etna or Vesuvius vulcanoes. When in Naples I plan to spend a day to see Pompeii and the acrhitecture.
I want to complete an epic climb up a 2000m mountain, or a bunch of them!
I'd like to try something new (via ferrata, horseback riding, or birdwatching?)...
I am looking forward to bike in the Dolomites, Sardinia, and many other breathtaking locations.
I do not expect this adventure to be life-changing, but I will do what I can to make it memorable. Keep my mind open.

## Difficulties

I expect to face challenges, some of them self-caused, some of them unforeseen. I will have to make tough decisions and prioritisations, and there will always be something more I wish I had packed for the journey. I will certainly not enjoy every moment. At times I will stress myself up even though I decide my own schedule. Not all people are well-meaning, and I need to keep my guard up and keep full attention to my belongings at all times. I expect myself to push the limits and suffer at times, but also relax and heal. I will feel stressed, nervous, and lost at times. Traffic may get dangerous and I might need to take a detour for safer roads. I will lose motivation and scream out in frustration. The weather will not always cooperate well. Some areas may become overwhelming, some areas may be dangerous. I might get sick or injured, and I might have to face the decision to cancel the journey.<br>

However, these <bo>difficulties add contrast</bo> to the journey. No good story was ever monotone and peaceful. And who doesn't love a good story?
