## The curiousity
For as long as I can remember, exploring new ground and learning new stuff has excited me. During my childhood me an my brother often accompanied our father with farmwork. Many days were spent in the tractor out on the fields, playing outside or feeding the cows. I also spent much time alone, immersing myself in an atlas or lexicon where I could learn anything about everything. This curiousness has always remained within me. 

## The passion for movement
I started track & field when I was 11, which has brought a lot of personal development and joy to this day. Track is an excellent sport for pushing your own limits, and experiencing first-hand how mental strength and discipline can help you reach achievements you once thought were impossible. I have since then gradually become a training freak, and reached my long-standing goal of competing in the national championships in August 2025. I am very proud of that!

## Newfound niche in Norway
The curiousity of exploring grew strong when I started my exchange semester in Norway 2022. I had 9 days free, in October and seied the opportunity. I found bikepacking bags to lease and strapped them onto my 50€ bike. Without a route in mind, I hopped on a ferry to Kristiansand and started from there. The first night I slept on a concrete floor inside a lighthouse, sheltered from the cold Norwegian autumn rain storm. Laying uncomfortably on the concrete listening to the rain I wondered if it was even worth continuing. But I convinced myself that it was only going to get better. So was it. The following seven days were spent on the saddle, zig-zagging my way through the Norwegian interior valleys past incredible locations like Åndalsnes, Geiranger, Flåm, and Stryn. Lots of fun surprises along the way. When I had returned to Trondheim I had a rich bank of memories hard-printed in the back of my brain. I still remember every hour of this journey four years later. Since then I crossed Sweden from south to north, which you can read more about in the section "Sweden 2024". That was also very memorable, but I couldn't keep myself from dreamin about crossing Italy some day in the future, now is the time!

## The passing
This heading has a double sense. When my father passed away from cancer, letting go of him physically was very challenging. The fact that he is no more with us is truly heartbreaking. Despite the inherent brutality of death, I find it motivating as well. What if the fact that we are alive and well is a source of gratefulness? Not all things in life are avoidable, only death is certain. <em>The sharpness of death brings meaning to life</em>, it provides reasons to be grateful. <br>The passing of time is another point of inspiration, or perhaps rather motivation. Death does not care about our plans for the future, each day people see their dreams shattered by unforeseen tragedies. Why would I wait for the 'perfect timing' to do this journey, when it is possible right here and now? Some people would give up everything just to breathe, see, or move normally. It feels ungrateful and rather stupid to let opportunity pass. I am halfway through my twenties already and it will likely not be easier to find room for a grand project like this.

## The italian language, nature and culture
The summer 2020 was supposed to be a summer on rails where I wanted to visit Italy. The pandemic forced me to cancel my plans, and motivated me to learn Italian for the next summer. I never ended up doing a eurorail trip but visited some friends in Italy in 2023. At that point I had learned enough to speak and comprehend italian, and I became hooked to the learning process. Since then, I have maintained a fairly good level. I am excited to resume my development in italian language, and to integrate myself as much as possible during this journey. Apart from learning more about the different dialects and cultural traits I am very curious to see how the food, nature, and general feeling changes across the regions in Italy.

## But what is it that actually gives meaning to this?
That is yet unknown! In my experience, it really comes down to what you make of it. There will be tough challenges. There will be open-minded people. And there will be unforgettable memories along the way. People talk about <em>finding meaning</em>, finding purpose, in life. However, I would claim life is about <em>creating</em> meaningful memories and befriending your passions. What would the older version of me wish me to do? The old me, who can neither stay sharp on an active day, nor ride a bike anymore... The answer is obvious: DO IT! Additionally, it means much to me to contribute to charity and possibly help people stay alive facing one of the most tragic forms of cancer.

## Why now, why not wait?
Life will not serve me up with such a thing as "perfect timing". I realise this journey demands sacrifices of different sorts that I am willing to take. I maximise vacation days and work hard to make this possible. This project is a once-in-a-lifetime journey that I am very fond of making as memorable as possible. My future self would not regret that I follow my dreams. La vita é bellissima!
