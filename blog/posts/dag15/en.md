.
This day started at the ferry 4:45, being woken up by the ferry voice announcement telling us to get ready for debarkation on Sicily.
At 6:00 I was on Sicilian ground for my first time, and started pedaling up Monte Pellegrino with an amazing view of the town and the sunrise! It was a great first impression of the island. Palermo is really big, with 630.000 inhabitants.
<br> I managed the traffic without much problems, and went to see the hectic Ballaró market and the lovely-looking Rosalía statue outside the Cathedral. 
After I finally found a drinkable-water fountain I set off southeast towards Agrigento. Today was very hot and I had to take a siesta during 13-15 to avoid the worst heat. I am only 170km away from Tunisia in Africa, and the sun is blazing hot!
As the evening came, I went up to a small village, 900 inhabitants. The very steep climb up to the village made me exhausted, and by the time I reached the village piazza I had to stop to recover.
I started taking with a group of signori in Italian and one thing led to another... After a while one of the men, Antonio, asked me to eat dinner at his house, he also offered me a place to sleep.
I was very honoured to be invited to his family, both his children's families were gathered for celebrating the birthday of his wife. And after I told them my birthday is tomorrow they insisted on celebrating my birthday as well.
Everyone sang 'Tanti auguri' and we ate tasty carbonara with tiramisu as dessert.
This evening I could experience the italian life, and it was all cozy and authentic. 

## How did my body feel?
Being awake and biking in scorching heat all day made my body very tired and my skin was fairly red, but I had an amazing sleep. Grazie mille!

## Best moment of the day
Taking, laughing and sharing some moments with these people who I just got to know. I am so grateful!

## Person I met
An car driver pulled down the car window and said Buona Giornata. It was at a time when I needed it. A guy in Lercara Friddi helped me find water when I was very very thirsty. And if course the Signori of Comitini for a nice welcome to your village.

## What did I eat?
Cannoli & Espresso for breakfast, Rosetta Bolognese for lunch, another Panino and pastry in the afternoon, and a big birthday meal with Tiramisu in the evening. 

## Landscape & nature
Sicily is completely different from Sardinia and Corsica. Much dryer and much more fields, typically wheat or grass, and much less forests. I am shocked by the elevation, just like Sardinia there are lots of hills and mountains scattered across the island.

[photo:2]

## Smells & details
Unfortunately, there is a lot of trash on the roadside, never seen so much. The water collection is a bit lacking, and many buildings are decadent. In general, things feel less maintained here than on Sardinia.

## How do I feel overall?
Tired but filled with happiness from the surprise of the day. I never expected to celebrate my 25th birthday with an entire Italian family tree of 10 people!
