# The day in short
The day started at the B&B in Butera where I was met with a splendid view and splendid weather. As the town sits perched on top of a hill, I could see very far.
I biked through the midday heat with serious protection against the sun: SPF 50 and a bandana. The first stop, Comiso, was reached later than planned but it was nice. I tasted some Sicilian products from a pasticciera.
Thereafter, I went over a hill and rolled down to Ragusa and it's old town Ragusa Ilbo. The town was heavily struck by the giant earthquake in 1692, and as a result much of the city was rebuilt in Sicilian baroque architecture. 
After yet another hill I made it to Modica where I ate a delicious Sicilian meal consisting if Gambacutto and Capotana. Rhey say "In Italia si mangia bene" whoch means that when you are in Italy you will often eat food that tastes good. This day was no exception!
There are so many regional dishes and varieties and they taste amazing. In Modica I walked into a chocolate affair to buy some chocolate. 
The cashier, Rossella, was a sympathetic and genuine person. Like many other people, she also outburst "Mamma mia" when I say I biked Corsica and Sardinia already by myself. She had such a positive energy and she kindly offered me a third chocolate bar to help me get through the day. Grazie mille, ancora una volta!
The day endes with difficulties finding a place to sleep for the night, but eventually found a very nice host.

## How did my body feel?
Strong throughout today's ride! But the climb between Cosimo and Ragusa was fatiguing midday with the strong sun.
