The day started at an agricamping. They had all animals I could imagine: Cows, sheep, donkeys, hens, dogs, cats, pigs, horses and even a might peacock! 
I bought a salami from the farmer and thanked him for a good talk the night before, he was happy I could talk Italian. Me too. 
By midday I reached Nuoro, a hilly city in the middle of Sardinia. Little did I know I was about to meet such an authentic and warm lady in the bakery.
Even though she had her hands full she still made time to talk and offered me some samples of the cookies and breads they were baking. I am not sure how your name is spelled but thank you Pasquina. 
After Nuoro I paid a visit to Olgosolo, famous for their murals.
Thereafter I visited a museum of the traditional masks in Mamoiada. This region has a lot of history that I never would have known hadn't I decided to go through the central part of Sardinia. Basically every year in January they are marching rhythmically, stomping the ground, wearing scary masks, sheep fur, and cow bells on their back in order to wake up mother nature for a good harvest this year. You can Google Mamoiada and read more.
<br> When biking through certain villages everyone stopped what they were doing to look at me. A gentle Buonasera and they are always replying with a smile. It feels a bit like I am a movie character. And yeah I am making my own story!
