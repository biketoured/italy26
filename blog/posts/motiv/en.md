# What makes this my dream adventure?
See this as a section where I try to justify my choices for my friends and relatives.<br><br>

[photo:1]

<strong>The freedom:</strong> I decide where to go and I can stop wherever and whenever I can. In fact, I am certain that I will modify the route lots of times. <br>
<strong>The language:</strong> Ever since I started learning Italian on my own I've been motivated to visit different parts of Italy and speak with locals.<br>
<strong>The challenge:</strong> A challenge like this feeds me motivation and a sense of accomplishment.<br>
<strong>The variation:</strong> Italian nature and culture is quite diverse, and I am curious to get a feel of the similarities and differences across the regions.<br>
<strong>The bikepacking:</strong> Bikepacking is an incredibly rewarding way to travel which I can talk about for hours. No other way of travelling gives time for processing the surroundings with all senses activated, all while being sound for the body and mind.<br>
<strong>The hospitality:</strong> Italians are delighted when a foreigner speaks Italian, and can open up in a way that feels authentic.<br>
<strong>The memories:</strong> An experience like this creates memories that I am sure I will keep close to my heart forever.<br>
<strong>The food:</strong> Do I really need to tell you more? <br>
<strong>The geography:</strong> Italy has a very variable geography, with environment shifting even in a day to day basis.
<strong>The friends:</strong> When on exchange in Norway I met some Italians that I'm hoping to reunite with along the trip.<br>
<strong>The culture:</strong> The italian culture is interesting to me, most definitively as a result of the above.<br>
<strong>The privilege.</strong> Thinking about how many people cannot do a journey like this makes me beyond grateful to even imagine it is possible. Tomorrow might not be possible. Life is fragile. Better do it now when I have the chance!
