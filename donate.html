// What we are fighting

The brain is the most complex structure in the known universe. Each day people around us are affected by different kinds of brain diseases. Tap any to learn more.

Brain tumours
Abnormal growths in or around the brain — from slow-growing benign tumours to aggressive glioblastomas with median survival under 15 months.
ADHD
A neurodevelopmental condition affecting attention and impulse control. One of the most common brain-based diagnoses, affecting children and adults alike.
Addiction
A chronic brain disorder involving compulsive substance use or behaviours despite their harmful consequences. Research links it to disrupted reward pathways in the brain.
Stroke
The second leading cause of death globally. Every minute without blood flow costs ~2 million neurons.
Multiple Sclerosis
An autoimmune disease attacking the myelin sheath around nerve fibres, disrupting signals between brain and body.
Depression
A mood disorder affecting hundreds of millions worldwide. Involves changes in brain chemistry and structure.
Alzheimer's
The leading cause of dementia. Gradually destroys memory and cognitive function. No cure exists yet.
Dementia
Loss of memory, language, and reasoning severe enough to affect daily life.
ALS
Rapidly destroys motor neurons controlling muscles. Most people survive 2–5 years after diagnosis. No cure exists.
